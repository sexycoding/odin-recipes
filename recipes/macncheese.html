Quick Killer Mac N' Cheese

Impress your friends and family with this easy and delicious recipe

This recipe is sure to impress. It takes a bit of know how to get the propotioning right, because it's mostly all eyeballing. Once you get it down you'll be able to crank out a delicious pot of mac n' cheese with no extra dishes in about 15 minutes flat.

Keep in mind this recipe is meant to be more like general guidlines. The ingredients do not need to be exact and you can use similar types depending on what you have around in your kitchen. Don't stress!

Absolutely Required Ingredients:

  • Macaroni (or some other kind of noodle)
  • Butter (or plant butter, but real butter is best)
  • Cheese
  • Milk (or milk substitute, I like oat milk!)
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • Mustard

Optional but really nice to have:

  • Garlic Salt (or powder or fresh cloves)
  • Red Pepper Flakes
  • Granulated Onion
  • Nutritional Yeast (if you dont want cheese)

I suggest pulling all your refrigerated ingredients out at the beginning of cooking so that they can come up to room temperature. Everything melts better if it's been warmed up a wee bit.

Steps:

  1. Fill a pot about 3/4 with water. Give a few shakes of salt into the water and then place on your stove to boil. I usually set the heat nice and high so that we can get a rolling boil going quickly.
  2. When the water is boiling fill to the water line with pasta. Cover and reduce heat by a few clicks to med high (or 8 on electric). Cook until pasta is just soft, about 10 minutes.
  3. Turn off heat and strain pasta. Quickly return pasta to the pot
  4. Cut off about 1/4 of a stick of butter and throw it on top of the noodles
  5. Throw on a few handfuls of cheese (or a handful of nutritional yeast if you arent doing any dairy). You want enough cheese to cover the noodles and then some. Create a small mound. DONT STIR YET!
  6. Put one squirt of mustard on top of the cheese.
  7. Do a few shakes of salt (or garlic salt if your fancy), and your other seasonings to your liking.
  8. Stir everything up, making sure the butter is melting. If you were too slow and things arent melting, put the pot back on the burner. If you have an electric stove, the residual heat from cooking is enough, otherwise turn the burner on the lowest setting you can.
  9. The cheese will probably look a little stringy, which is ok. Add a few splashes of milk (or milk substitute) and start stirring it in. Keep adding milk in small amounts and stirring gently until the sauce is the consistency you like.
  10. If you accidently add too much milk, just throw a fine layer of nutritional yeast on top and stir it in. It adds a nice flavor and will help thicken things up!

Enjoy!
